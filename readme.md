# CTRL+SAFE

CTRL+SAFE is an interactive cybersecurity awareness website designed to educate teenagers aged 13–18 about online grooming, online predators, and safe digital behaviour.

The project focuses on helping teenagers recognise suspicious online behaviour, understand grooming tactics, and learn practical safety steps they can take when they feel unsafe online.

## Project Overview

In today’s digital environment, teenagers frequently interact through social media, online games, messaging platforms, and other online communities. While these platforms can be useful and entertaining, they can also expose young users to online predators.

CTRL+SAFE was developed as a cybersecurity awareness initiative to make online safety education more engaging, practical, and easy to understand.

Instead of only presenting information through text, this website uses visual sections, safety tips, and interactive game-based learning to help teenagers identify red flags and respond safely.

## Objectives

The main objectives of CTRL+SAFE are:

- To educate teenagers about online grooming and predatory behaviour.
- To help users recognise common grooming red flags.
- To teach practical online safety steps.
- To provide Malaysia-based reporting information.
- To make cybersecurity awareness more interactive and memorable.
- To encourage teenagers to seek help from trusted adults when facing online threats.

## Key Features

### 1. About This Initiative

This section explains the purpose of CTRL+SAFE and introduces how online grooming can happen through online conversations, social media, and gaming platforms.

It also includes infographic-style cards that highlight the main learning goals:

- Recognise
- Think
- Protect
- Report

### 2. Online Grooming Red Flags

This section explains common warning signs of grooming behaviour, such as:

- Excessive attention or flattery
- Asking for secrecy
- Requesting personal information
- Offering gifts or rewards
- Moving conversations to private platforms
- Using pressure, guilt, or threats

### 3. CTRL+SAFE Safety Steps

This section provides practical steps teenagers can follow when facing suspicious online behaviour:

1. Stop replying
2. Screenshot evidence
3. Block the person
4. Report the account or incident
5. Tell a trusted adult

The section also includes Malaysia-based reporting channels such as:

- Talian Kasih
- Cyber999
- Police report

### 4. Interactive Game: Would You Trust Them?

The website includes an interactive game page where users can practise identifying unsafe online situations.

The game includes:

- Chat Challenge  
  A simulated online conversation where users choose safe or unsafe responses.

- Spot the Red Flag  
  A mini game where users identify suspicious signs in fake social media profiles.

- Safety Quiz 
  An Interactive safety quiz with instant feedback .



These activities help users practise decision-making in realistic online situations.

Educational Value

CTRL+SAFE helps users understand that online grooming may not always appear dangerous at first. Predators may use kindness, attention, gifts, emotional support, or secrecy to slowly gain trust.

By using interactive examples, the website helps teenagers practise recognising these behaviours before they face them in real life.

Safety Message

If an online conversation feels uncomfortable, threatening, secretive, or manipulative, teenagers should not handle it alone.

The recommended action is:

Stop. Screenshot. Block. Report. Tell a trusted adult.
Disclaimer

This website is created for educational and cybersecurity awareness purposes only. It does not replace professional, legal, or emergency support.

If someone is in immediate danger, they should contact a trusted adult, local authorities, or emergency services.
